What brings people to psychotherapy in Derby

Most people who come to AIP Therapy are not in crisis. They are functioning — going to work, managing their relationships, getting through the day. But something is not right. There is a persistent weight they cannot shake. A pattern that keeps repeating. A version of themselves they know exists but cannot seem to reach.

Psychotherapy is not about being broken. It is about understanding what is driving your experience and building the capacity to respond differently — with less suffering, more clarity and more control.

Common reasons people seek psychotherapy

  • Anxiety, panic attacks and chronic worry
  • Trauma responses and PTSD
  • Stress, burnout and emotional exhaustion
  • Low mood, depression and emotional flatness
  • Sleep problems and recurring nightmares
  • Low confidence and persistent self-doubt
  • Anger, emotional overwhelm and reactivity
  • Relationship difficulties and communication breakdown
  • Work stress and performance anxiety
  • ADHD-related emotional and behavioural challenges
  • Overthinking, decision fatigue and avoidance
  • Habits and patterns that feel impossible to change

How integrative psychotherapy works at AIP

James Harris is a BACP-registered integrative psychotherapist. Integrative means the work is not confined to a single model — it draws on whichever frameworks are most useful for you, at this stage, with these specific challenges.

The goal is not endless talking without direction. It is practical progress — understanding what is driving your experience, and building real capacity to respond differently.

Psychodynamic understanding

Exploring the deeper patterns, early experiences and unconscious processes that shape how you think, feel and behave — without getting lost in the past at the expense of the present.

CBT-informed tools

Practical techniques for identifying and shifting unhelpful thought patterns, managing anxiety responses and building more effective behavioural habits.

Hypnotherapy integration

Where appropriate, hypnotherapy is integrated to work with subconscious patterns — particularly useful for anxiety, sleep disruption, confidence and deeply ingrained habits.

NLP-informed techniques

Reframing the internal narrative, shifting limiting beliefs and building more useful mental models for how you relate to yourself and the world around you.

What a psychotherapy session actually looks like

Many people put off starting therapy because they are not sure what to expect. Here is what a session at AIP Therapy in Derby involves.

Before you start — free intro call

A 15-minute call to talk through what you are dealing with, ask any questions and check whether working together makes sense. No commitment required.

First session — assessment and direction

A full 50-minute session to explore your history, current challenges and goals. James will outline a suggested approach and you will agree on how to proceed together.

Ongoing sessions — structured progress

Regular sessions with a clear focus each time. Progress is reviewed openly. The approach adapts as your needs change. You are always in control of the pace and direction.

Psychotherapy in Derby — frequently asked questions

What is psychotherapy and how does it work?

Psychotherapy is a structured, confidential talking therapy that helps you understand the patterns driving your thoughts, feelings and behaviour. At AIP, sessions combine psychotherapy, counselling, hypnotherapy and NLP tools as needed — with a focus on practical progress.

What can psychotherapy help with?

James works with anxiety, panic attacks, trauma, PTSD, stress, burnout, low mood, sleep problems, low confidence, anger, relationship pressure, ADHD-related difficulties and emotional overwhelm.

How much does psychotherapy cost in Derby?

Sessions are currently offered at £67 (normally £110) for a limited period. Fees are confirmed before booking.

Do you offer in-person psychotherapy in Derby?

Yes. In-person sessions are available at Room 6, 72 Wilson Street, Derby DE1 1PL — a private, calm clinic in Derby city centre.

Do you offer online psychotherapy?

Yes. Secure online sessions are available across the UK via video call — ideal for clients in Derbyshire and beyond.

How long is each session?

Sessions are 50 minutes. Frequency is usually weekly or fortnightly, matched to your goals and capacity.

How many sessions will I need?

It varies. Many clients report meaningful progress in the first 3–6 sessions. Longer-term support is available for more complex presentations.

Is psychotherapy confidential?

Yes. Sessions are fully confidential in line with BACP ethical standards, with standard safeguarding exceptions explained clearly at the outset.

What is integrative psychotherapy?

Integrative psychotherapy draws on multiple frameworks — psychodynamic, CBT, person-centred and humanistic — rather than a single rigid model. This allows the work to be tailored to you specifically.

How do I book?

Contact us via the website, phone or WhatsApp to arrange a free 15-minute intro call. We will check availability and answer any questions before you commit.
