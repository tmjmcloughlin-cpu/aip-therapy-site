AEO in 2026 (UK): the playbook for winning answer boxes and AI summaries

AEO in 2026 is about format discipline. Not “write more content”. Not “add more keywords”. It’s: structure information so a machine can extract the answer confidently, then give the user a reason to continue.

The 4 layers you’re optimising for

  • Classic blue links (still matters)
  • Featured snippets (direct answers)
  • People Also Ask (question expansion)
  • AI summary layers (where Google/others summarise)

Answer-first structure (copy this)

  1. One-sentence definition (what is it?)
  2. 40–80 word answer block (direct, complete, plain English)
  3. 3–7 bullet steps (if it’s a process)
  4. Examples (UK context, costs, edge cases)
  5. Next-step CTA (what should they do now?)

What wins in the UK specifically

  • Clear pricing expectations (ranges are fine)
  • Compliance/professional signals where relevant (accreditations, standards)
  • Location clarity (where you operate, response times, service areas)

How to choose AEO targets (stop guessing)

Open Search Console and look for queries that are already close:

  • Positions 4–15
  • High impressions, low CTR
  • Question-based phrasing: what/why/how/can/should

Then rewrite the page to be answer-first. One page at a time. Measured weekly.
