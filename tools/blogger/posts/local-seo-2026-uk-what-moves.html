Local SEO in 2026: what actually moves the needle (UK)

Local SEO hasn’t become “mystical” in 2026 — it’s just less tolerant of lazy execution. If you’re a UK local business, here’s what reliably drives results.

1) One great service page beats ten “SEO pages”

Pick your top service and build one page that a customer would genuinely trust:

  • What you do (plain English)
  • Who it’s for / who it isn’t for
  • Pricing expectations (ranges are fine)
  • Proof: reviews, case notes, before/after, credentials, guarantees
  • Local coverage: the towns you actually serve

2) Google Business Profile is still a ranking system

  • Correct primary category
  • Services filled properly
  • Fresh photos
  • Posts you can maintain
  • Consistent NAP across the web

3) Reviews: the compounding lever

Reviews improve conversions and often lift CTR in Maps/local packs. Make review collection a process, not a hope.

4) Local authority that isn’t “SEO link building”

Sponsorships, associations, partnerships, local PR. If it would still make sense without Google, it’s probably a good idea.

5) Track weekly, decide monthly

  • GSC: clicks, impressions, top pages, top queries
  • GBP: calls, website clicks, direction requests
  • Leads: count + quality notes

That’s the work. The rest is just consistency.
