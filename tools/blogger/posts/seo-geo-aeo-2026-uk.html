SEO vs GEO vs AEO in 2026 (UK): what’s changed, what hasn’t

People are arguing about acronyms again. Fine. Here’s the practical way I think about it in 2026:

  • SEO = get found in Google’s traditional results (pages + products + local packs).
  • AEO (Answer Engine Optimisation) = win the answer layer: featured snippets, “People also ask”, AI Overviews style summaries, and any SERP element that tries to finish the query without a click.
  • GEO (Generative Engine Optimisation) = get recommended by AI search assistants and LLM-powered discovery: “best X for Y”, comparison queries, tool lists, vendor recommendations, and brand mentions that drive action.

Different surfaces. Same reality: the winner is the business that looks real, specific, and trustworthy.

What hasn’t changed (this is still 80% of the game)

  • Relevance: do you clearly match the query and intent?
  • Authority: do trusted sources mention you, cite you, and link to you?
  • Trust: would a human believe your site and your offer?
  • Usability: can someone act (call / book / buy) without friction?

What’s changed in 2026 (where people are getting caught out)

  • Generic content is filtered harder. If your page could be swapped with 50 competitors, you’re not building anything Google (or AI) wants to recommend.
  • Entity signals matter more. Consistent brand details, real-world signals, and clear topical focus beat “SEO trick” pages.
  • Answers steal demand. You need to structure pages so you can win the answer box and still earn the click for deeper intent.
  • Brand-led search increases. People are increasingly searching for “the one they’ve heard of”. GEO is as much positioning as it is optimisation.

The UK angle: what I’d do first for a local business

  1. Fix your core service pages (clear offer + pricing expectations + proof).
  2. Lock in Google Business Profile hygiene and review velocity.
  3. Build one credible content hub (FAQs / guides) that supports your money pages.
  4. Earn local mentions (partnerships, sponsorships, associations) that exist outside SEO.
  5. Track a weekly scorecard so you can prove movement.
