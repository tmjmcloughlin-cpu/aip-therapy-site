GEO in 2026: how to get recommended by AI search (without ‘AI content spam’)

If SEO is about ranking, GEO is about being recommended. That recommendation tends to go to brands that feel real: specific positioning, proof, and consistent signals across the web.

What actually drives AI recommendations

  • Clarity: who you help, what you do, where you do it
  • Proof: reviews, case studies, testimonials, outcomes
  • Consensus: multiple trusted sources saying the same thing about you
  • Consistency: name/offer/details match everywhere (site, socials, directories)

The easy GEO wins

  • Create one “best page” per core topic (not ten thin pages)
  • Write comparison content where you can be the obvious choice
  • Get mentioned by real organisations (local/industry) — not link farms
  • Make your author/company identity explicit (who is behind this?)

GEO isn’t separate from SEO — it’s the same credibility, expressed differently

If a human wouldn’t recommend you after reading your site, an AI assistant shouldn’t either. Build for humans first, then structure it so machines can verify it.
