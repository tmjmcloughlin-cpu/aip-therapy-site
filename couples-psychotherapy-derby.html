What couples therapy can help with

Relationships go through difficult periods. Whether you are experiencing repeated conflict, growing emotional distance or a specific crisis, couples psychotherapy provides a structured, confidential space to work through it together.

  • Communication breakdown and recurring arguments
  • Trust issues, jealousy and recovery after infidelity
  • Emotional distance and feeling disconnected
  • Intimacy difficulties and physical or emotional withdrawal
  • Conflict over parenting, finances or life decisions
  • Relationship anxiety and fear of abandonment
  • Stress from major life transitions (new baby, bereavement, redundancy)
  • Patterns from past relationships or childhood affecting the present
  • Considering separation and wanting to make an informed decision
  • Wanting to strengthen a good relationship before problems escalate

Sessions are available in Derby city centre and online across the UK, so support is accessible wherever you are.

How couples psychotherapy works in Derby

Couples psychotherapy is not about taking sides or assigning blame. The process is collaborative: both partners are heard, and the therapist works to help you understand the patterns driving conflict and disconnection.

Sessions draw on integrative psychotherapy, which means the approach is tailored to you as a couple rather than following a rigid script. This can include exploring how your individual histories — family background, past relationships, attachment styles — are showing up in your current dynamic.

Understanding patterns

Many couples find themselves having the same argument repeatedly without resolution. Therapy helps identify the underlying emotional triggers and communication styles that keep the cycle going.

Rebuilding communication

You will develop practical tools to express needs clearly, listen differently and respond rather than react — skills that continue to work long after sessions end.

Reconnecting emotionally

Emotional distance often builds gradually. Therapy creates space to re-establish safety and closeness, whether the goal is to repair the relationship or to navigate a transition with clarity.

Who couples therapy in Derby is for

Couples psychotherapy is not only for relationships in crisis. It is for any couple who want to understand each other better, communicate more effectively or work through a specific challenge.

  • Couples who argue frequently without resolution
  • Partners who feel emotionally distant or disconnected
  • Couples navigating infidelity or a breach of trust
  • Partners with different communication styles causing friction
  • Couples under pressure from external stressors (work, family, finances)
  • Couples considering separation who want to make a considered decision
  • Couples who want to strengthen their relationship proactively
  • Same-sex couples, unmarried partners and couples at any life stage

Integrative approach to couples psychotherapy

Sessions at AIP Therapy integrate several evidence-informed frameworks depending on what the couple needs:

Psychodynamic insight

Exploring how past experiences and attachment patterns are influencing present-day conflict and emotional responses within the relationship.

Emotionally Focused Therapy (EFT) principles

Identifying the emotional needs and fears driving negative interaction cycles, and creating new patterns of connection and responsiveness.

Cognitive and behavioural tools

Practical communication strategies and behavioural changes that both partners can implement between sessions to reinforce progress.

Hypnotherapy and NLP (where appropriate)

For individuals within the couple who are carrying anxiety, trauma responses or entrenched patterns, focused hypnotherapy or NLP techniques can accelerate change.

Online sessions are available across the UK, as well as in-person appointments in Derby city centre.

What to expect from your first session

The first session is an opportunity for both partners to share their perspective and for the therapist to understand the relationship history and current concerns. There is no pressure to have everything figured out before you arrive.

  • 50-minute session, in-person in Derby or online
  • Both partners heard without judgement or taking sides
  • Initial goals and expectations discussed openly
  • Confidentiality arrangements for couples work explained
  • A clear sense of how future sessions will be structured

A free 15-minute introductory call is available before booking, so you can ask questions and decide if this feels right.

Couples therapy Derby — frequently asked questions

What is couples psychotherapy in Derby?

Couples psychotherapy in Derby is a structured, professional process where both partners work with a therapist to explore relationship patterns, improve communication and resolve conflict. Sessions are available in-person at Room 6, 72 Wilson St, Derby DE1 1PL and online across the UK.

Do you offer online couples therapy?

Yes. Secure online sessions are available for couples across the UK, including those outside Derby.

What issues can couples therapy help with?

Common issues include communication breakdown, trust and infidelity, conflict cycles, emotional distance, intimacy difficulties, parenting stress, life transitions and relationship anxiety.

How long is each couples session?

Most couples sessions are 50 minutes, with frequency agreed to match your goals and capacity.

Is couples therapy confidential?

Yes. Sessions are confidential in line with UK professional and ethical standards. Confidentiality arrangements specific to couples work are discussed and agreed at the outset.

Do both partners need to attend?

Typically both partners attend joint sessions. Individual sessions may also be arranged where appropriate, and this is discussed openly at the start.

How many sessions will we need?

It varies by goal and relationship history. Many couples report meaningful progress in the first 4–8 sessions, with longer-term work available for deeper patterns.
