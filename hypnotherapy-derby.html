What clinical hypnotherapy actually is — and is not

Most people's idea of hypnotherapy comes from stage shows — someone clucking like a chicken, or being made to do something embarrassing. Clinical hypnotherapy is nothing like that. You remain fully aware and in complete control throughout every session. You cannot be made to do or say anything you do not want to.

What hypnotherapy does is create a state of focused relaxation — sometimes called a trance — in which the subconscious mind becomes more receptive to change. The subconscious is where your automatic thoughts, emotional responses and ingrained habits live. Talking therapy works well with the conscious mind. Hypnotherapy works with both.

At AIP Therapy in Derby, hypnotherapy is never used in isolation. It is integrated with psychotherapy, CBT-informed tools and NLP techniques — creating a structured, evidence-informed approach that addresses both the surface behaviour and the underlying pattern driving it.

What hypnotherapy can help with in Derby

Anxiety & panic

Hypnotherapy works directly with the subconscious threat-response that drives anxiety and panic — reducing the automatic alarm reaction and building a calmer baseline.

Sleep problems

Racing thoughts, difficulty switching off, broken sleep and early waking all respond well to hypnotherapy. Sessions work with the patterns keeping the nervous system in a high-alert state.

Smoking cessation

One of hypnotherapy's strongest evidence areas. Sessions address the subconscious associations and habitual triggers that make stopping difficult — not just the physical craving.

Confidence & self-esteem

Working with the internal narrative — the critical voice that says you are not good enough — and replacing it with something more accurate and more useful at a subconscious level.

Phobias & fears

Specific phobias — flying, needles, social situations, driving — respond well to hypnotherapy-based desensitisation combined with psychotherapy.

Stress & burnout

Hypnotherapy helps regulate the chronic stress response — reducing cortisol-driven reactivity and building the capacity to recover between demands.

Habits & behaviour change

Whether it is overeating, nail biting, avoidance or any other ingrained habit — hypnotherapy works at the level where habits are stored and maintained.

PTSD & trauma

Used carefully and in combination with stabilisation-first psychotherapy, hypnotherapy can support trauma processing and reduce the intrusive symptoms of PTSD.

Hypnotherapy in Derby — frequently asked questions

What is clinical hypnotherapy?

Clinical hypnotherapy uses a state of focused relaxation to work with the subconscious mind — the part that drives automatic thoughts, habits and emotional responses. At AIP it is integrated with psychotherapy and CBT tools for a structured, evidence-informed approach.

Will I lose control during hypnotherapy?

No. You remain fully aware and in control throughout every session. Hypnotherapy is a collaborative process — you cannot be made to do or say anything you do not want to.

Does hypnotherapy actually work?

Yes, for the right presentations. Hypnotherapy has a strong evidence base for anxiety, sleep, IBS, pain management and habit change. At AIP it is integrated with psychotherapy, which improves outcomes further.

What can hypnotherapy help with?

Anxiety, panic, sleep problems, smoking cessation, confidence, phobias, stress, burnout, PTSD, ADHD-related patterns, habits and emotional regulation.
